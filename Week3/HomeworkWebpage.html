Three SQL Concepts


Normalization

Normalization has many forms but the first three are what is used by many.

  1. 1NF: First Normal Form
    • Table is broken into its smallest component or atomic components.
    • Primary keys are required
  2. 2NF: Second Normal Form
    • Must have 1NF
    • Must not have partial dependencies.
        No clolumns in the table should depend of part of the primary key,
        usually involves composite primary keys
  3. 3NF: Third Normal Form
    • Must have 2NF
    • Must not have transitive dependencies
        The primary key of the table shouldn't depend on any other table column
        that also depends on another table
